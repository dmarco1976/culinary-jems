## About Discord (Don't Worry, It's Easy)

Okay so Discord — I literally just started learning it myself the other day. It's basically a chat app, like a mix between texting and Slack. You download it on your phone and/or computer, and you'll have your own private server called "Culinary JEMs Marketing."

Think of it like a group chat with different rooms (they call them "channels"). You have four:

- **#approvals** — Where your weekly content plan shows up every Sunday. This is your main check-in spot.
- **#content-queue** — When a new draft is ready (like when you upload a photo), it pops up here.
- **#notifications** — Confirmations when posts go live, plus alerts if someone comments on your posts.
- **#commands** — Where you type commands to the bot (like "make me a hype post for Goldwater Brewing tonight").

That's it. You don't need to learn anything complicated about Discord. You open it, check your channels, and type commands when you want something. The bot responds right there in the chat.

## Your Weekly Routine

This is the whole thing. About 15 minutes a week, total.

### Sunday Morning (5 minutes)

1. Open Discord on your phone
2. Check **#approvals** — your weekly content plan is there
3. Open the Content Calendar (it's a Google Sheet in your culinaryjems@gmail.com Drive)
4. Look through the draft posts for the week
5. For each one:
   - **Like it?** Change the Status column from "draft" to "approved"
   - **Want to tweak it?** Edit the caption right in the sheet — whatever you type is what gets posted
   - **Don't want it?** Change Status to "skipped"

That's your Sunday. Five minutes, coffee in hand, done.

### During the Week (2 minutes per photo)

When you're at an event:
- Snap 1-2 photos (food close-ups, the truck, crowd vibes — whatever looks good)
- Open Google Drive on your phone
- Drop the photos in the **"CulinaryJEMs Photos"** folder

Within about 5 minutes, the system will:
- Look at your photo
- Write a caption that matches the vibe
- Add it as a draft to your Content Calendar
- Ping you in Discord so you know it's there

You don't have to do anything else unless you want to review or edit the caption.

### Event Days — Friday/Saturday (0 minutes)

On days you have an event, the system automatically:
- Creates a "TONIGHT!" hype post at 9 AM
- Generates a branded story graphic with the venue and time
- Queues it to post at 10 AM

You don't have to touch anything. If you want to review it first, you'll get a Discord notification with a preview.

## How Posts Actually Go Out

Right now, we're in **manual mode** while we wait for Meta (Instagram/Facebook's parent company) to approve our app. This approval takes 1-4 weeks. Here's what that means:

**Right now (manual mode):**
- When a post is due, you'll get a Discord message with the caption and image
- You copy the caption, save the image, and post it yourself through Instagram/Facebook
- Or you can use Meta Business Suite (it's free) to schedule posts ahead of time

**Once Meta approves us (auto mode):**
- Approved posts will automatically publish to Instagram and Facebook at the scheduled time
- You'll get a Discord notification confirming it went out
- You don't have to touch anything — fully hands-off

Either way, **nothing ever posts without your approval.** The system only publishes posts you've marked as "approved" in the Content Calendar.

## The Content Calendar (Your Google Sheet)

This is your command center. It's a Google Sheet in your culinaryjems@gmail.com Drive called "Culinary JEMs — Content Calendar." Each row is one social media post.

The columns you care about:

| Column | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Status** | "draft" = needs your review. "approved" = will post. "posted" = already went out. "skipped" = you passed on it. |
| **Scheduled Date** | When it'll post |
| **Scheduled Time** | What time (Arizona time) |
| **Platform** | Instagram, Facebook, or both |
| **Caption** | The full post text with hashtags — **edit this anytime** |
| **Image URL** | Link to the graphic or photo |

**The only thing you really need to do here is change "draft" to "approved."** Everything else is optional.

Want to add your own post that the system didn't create? Just add a new row, fill in the status ("approved"), date, time, platform, and caption. Done.

## If Something Goes Wrong

### "I didn't get my weekly plan on Sunday"
Check Discord **#approvals** — it should arrive around 8 AM. If it's not there, type `!week` in **#commands** and it'll generate one on the spot.

### "A post didn't go out"
Open the Content Calendar. Is the status set to "approved"? (Not still "draft"?) Is the date and time correct? Check Discord **#notifications** for any error messages.

### "The AI wrote something I don't like"
Just edit the Caption cell in the Google Sheet. Whatever you type there is exactly what gets posted. Your version always wins over the AI's draft.

### "I want to post something that's not in the plan"
Add a new row to the Content Calendar. Fill in the status ("approved"), the date, time, platform, and your caption. The system will pick it up and post it.

### "I'm confused or something is broken"
Text Dominic. He can check the system from anywhere and fix things remotely. That's the beauty of the setup — you don't need to troubleshoot anything technical.
