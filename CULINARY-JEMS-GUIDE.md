### What Makes This Different

This isn't a generic chatbot. This is a **marketing agent** - think of it like hiring a real person to run your social media, except it never sleeps, never forgets, and gets better over time.

**Treat it like you'd treat a real marketing hire.** If you'd expect a human assistant to remember that you do events at Goldwater Brewing every other Friday - expect the same from the bot. If you'd be annoyed that your marketing person forgot the name of your best-selling slider - be annoyed at the bot too. Hold it to that standard.

The only things the bot *can't* do:
- **Read your mind** - you have to tell it what you want
- **Take photos** - you snap the pics, it does the rest
- **Run the food truck - that's Brian's domain 🍔

Everything else? As automated as possible.

### It Learns From You

Here's the really cool part: **everything you do teaches the bot.** Every caption you edit, every post you approve or skip, every photo you upload, every preference you mention - it all gets learned and incorporated into future marketing. The more you use it, the more it sounds like you and knows what works for your audience. It's constantly getting smarter about your brand.

The bot also stays current on **marketing industry best practices** - new social media features, trending content formats, what's working in the food truck space right now. It's not just doing what worked last month - it's evolving.

## Your Weekly Routine

About 15 minutes a week. And keep in mind - you don't have events every day. Most weeks it's a couple of events, so some days there's literally nothing for you to do. The system handles the content rotation on its own.

### Sunday Morning (5 minutes)

1. Open Discord on your phone
2. Check **#approvals** - your weekly content plan is there
3. Open the Content Calendar (Google Sheet in your Drive)
4. Look through the draft posts:
   - **Like it?** Change Status from "draft" to "approved"
   - **Want to tweak it?** Edit the caption - your version wins
   - **Don't want it?** Change Status to "skipped"

Done. Coffee in hand.

### Event Days (2 minutes per photo)

When you're at an event:
- Snap 1-2 photos (food close-ups, the truck, crowd vibes)
- Drop them in the **"CulinaryJEMs Photos"** folder on Google Drive

Within about 5 minutes, the system will:
- Look at your photo
- Write a caption that matches the vibe
- Add it as a draft to your Content Calendar
- Ping you in Discord

### Auto-Generated Event Posts (0 minutes)

On event days, the system automatically:
- Creates a "TONIGHT!" hype post at 9 AM
- Generates a branded story graphic with the venue and time
- Queues it to post at 10 AM

You don't touch anything. If you want to review first, check Discord for a preview.

### Non-Event Days

The system keeps your feed active even when you're not at an event - menu spotlights, community shoutouts, behind-the-scenes content. It handles the rotation. You just approve on Sunday and let it run.

## Catering - Your Growth Engine

Let's be real - catering is where the money is. The food truck is great for visibility and brand building, but catering is where Culinary JEMs can really scale. Every pop-up event is basically a live advertisement for your catering services. The system is built to help you turn that attention into booked gigs.

### How Leads Come In

When someone fills out the catering form on culinaryjems.com, three things happen instantly:

1. **You get an email** at culinaryjems@gmail.com with all the details (name, event type, headcount, date, location, message)
2. **It logs to your Google Sheet** ("Culinary JEMs - Form Submissions") so nothing falls through the cracks
3. **The bot pings you on Discord** so you see it right away, even if you're busy

### Your Lead Tracker (Google Sheet)

The Google Sheet isn't just a log - it's your sales pipeline. Every submission gets a row with:

| Column | What it is |
|--------|-----------|
| Timestamp | When they reached out |
| Name | Who they are |
| Email | How to reach them |
| Phone | Their number |
| Event Type | Corporate, wedding, birthday, etc. |
| Headcount | How many people |
| Preferred Date | When they want you |
| Location | Where the event is |
| Details | Their message |

Add your own columns to track the pipeline:
- **Status** - New, Contacted, Quote Sent, Booked, Declined
- **Follow-up Date** - When to check back
- **Quote Amount** - What you quoted them
- **Notes** - Any details from your conversations
- **Deposit Received** - Yes/No

### Follow-Up Workflow

Speed matters with leads. Here's the system:

**Within 2 hours of a new lead:**
- The bot drafts a personalized response based on their inquiry details
- You review it, tweak if needed, and send from culinaryjems@gmail.com
- The bot updates the Sheet status to "Contacted"

**If no response in 3 days:**
- The bot reminds you to follow up
- It drafts a friendly check-in email
- You send it or skip it

**If no response in 7 days:**
- One final follow-up draft from the bot
- After that, the lead goes to "No Response" status
- No one likes being pestered - three touches is enough

### What You Can Ask the Bot

| What you say | What happens |
|--------------|-------------|
| "New catering leads?" | Shows any unresponded inquiries |
| "Draft a response for the Johnson wedding" | Writes a personalized reply based on their form data |
| "Quote template for 50 people" | Generates a quote based on your pricing |
| "Follow-up reminder for next week" | Sets a reminder to check back on pending leads |
| "How many catering leads this month?" | Quick stats from your pipeline |
| "Catering promo post" | Creates a social media post promoting catering services |

### Building the Catering Brand

The marketing system supports catering growth in a few ways:

- **Catering highlight posts** - The bot creates social posts showcasing past catering events (you provide photos)
- **Testimonial posts** - Happy client quotes turned into branded graphics
- **Seasonal promos** - "Book your holiday party now" or "Wedding season is here" posts at the right times
- **The website catering page** - Professional, with a form that actually works and tracks leads

The more catering events you do and the more photos you share, the more content the bot has to work with. Every gig feeds the marketing flywheel.

## Troubleshooting

### "I didn't get my weekly plan on Sunday"
Ask the bot: "Generate this week's content." If the bot is offline, create posts manually in Meta Business Suite.

### "A post didn't go out"
Check the Content Calendar - is status set to "approved"? Is the date/time correct? Check Discord for error messages. If the bot is down, post manually.

### "The AI wrote something I don't like"
Edit the Caption in the Google Sheet. Your version always wins - and the bot learns from your edits.

### "I want to post something not in the plan"
Add a new row to the Content Calendar with status "approved." Or just post directly through Instagram/Facebook.

### "The bot isn't responding"
It might be a temporary outage. Wait 15-30 minutes and try again. If it's still down, text Dominic. In the meantime, use Meta Business Suite.

### "I'm confused or something is broken"
Text Dominic. He can check the system from anywhere and fix things remotely.
